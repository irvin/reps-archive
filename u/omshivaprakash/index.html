I'm a proud Kannadiga from Bangalore, Karnataka, India. I have been contributing to FOSS projects in various capacities for over a decade. I contribute to content creation on Wikipedia for Kannada language (kn), I also contribute to Wiki projects on tech aspects as a sysop. I have been a Mozillans working on KN localisation and I’m also a Mozilla Rep from Bengaluru, India. Founder member of Sanchaya - (a collective exploring the possibilities of using technology in linguistics & literary research) and Sanchi Foundation (“Sanchi” is a not for profit organisation working for the documentation of audio-visual heritage.). Vachana Sanchaya - https://vachana.sanchaya.net is one of the very much structured linguistic research tool built in Kannada through Sanchaya's initiatives.

I have been involved in community building activities around Kannada & Technology. My engagements like Gnu/Linux Habba, Arivina Alegalu, Hejje has engaged young minds towards language technology. I write technology articles in kannada on his blog Linuxaayana.net.

Apart from being active around technology sphere, he has immense interest around literature, photography, trekking, cycling etc. With over 17+ years of experience in the field of Information & Technology, he has played significant role as a trainer, Infrastructure Management Specialist, CTO, Director for Business Development & Technology etc.

Become a Community Coordinator in Your Region!

The Mozilla Reps program is open to all Mozillians who are 18 years of age and above. Before you become a Mozilla Rep, you must follow our learning process so that you can become a good Community Coordinator in your region.

Community Coordinators are not a power structure over other Mozillians, instead they are here to help, empower and motivate other Mozillians to contribute to Mozilla projects.

There are tons of opportunities you can benefit from joining this program:

  • Access to a global community which includes people from many different countries and cultural backgrounds.
  • Direct support from Mozilla to help you empower others in your region to advance Mozilla’s goals.
  • Opportunity to help your local community grow.
  • Grow your leadership and communication skills and build networks with new people from many different countries around the globe.
  • Amplify Mozilla’s impact in your local region and functional area.

If you want to learn more about our program, you might want to connect with one of our currently active Mozilla Reps in your region, so you can learn and understand the challenges and opportunities of this program.

  • The applicant needs to be at least 18 years old
  • The applicant needs to be active within Mozilla for at least 6 months
