Agenda

This meetup will serve as a SCRUM for the upcoming months so that the contributors can work in more Productive Way. One of the major issue faced is newbies want to contribute but don't know how to. So the focus will also be on explaining the various contribution verticals (also a hands on) to all the club leads and other attendees, so that they can explain the same in their clubs and eventually increase the individual contribution to the vertical in which they are comfortable. Contributors around Rajasthan are doing great but are random. For that we need to meet at a nice place where we can interact with each other and share the problems and find solutions and eventually start contributing in right direction..

Few Contribution verticals which will be discussed are-

  • Writing/Editing a MDN doc

  • Reporting and fixing bugs at Bugzilla

  • Army of Awesome

  • Localization

  • Creating Makes

  • Creating Demos

  • Support Forum

  • An overview of Firefox codebase and also Gaia (only for interested ones)

    The launch of Firefox OS is just in the corner we will have an hour of an app dev session(including a live app submission), in which we will have a basic app building session(using Appmaker tool) and an advanced dev session too(for advanced developers, including web apis etc.) Its the maker party season, the agenda also includes planning for few more parties and prepare the newly joined FSAs to take the leadership for such parties.

    We here in rajasthan are trying to build a group of awesome contributors/developers on which Mozilla can count.

Become a Community Coordinator in Your Region!

The Mozilla Reps program is open to all Mozillians who are 18 years of age and above. Before you become a Mozilla Rep, you must follow our learning process so that you can become a good Community Coordinator in your region.

Community Coordinators are not a power structure over other Mozillians, instead they are here to help, empower and motivate other Mozillians to contribute to Mozilla projects.

There are tons of opportunities you can benefit from joining this program:

  • Access to a global community which includes people from many different countries and cultural backgrounds.
  • Direct support from Mozilla to help you empower others in your region to advance Mozilla’s goals.
  • Opportunity to help your local community grow.
  • Grow your leadership and communication skills and build networks with new people from many different countries around the globe.
  • Amplify Mozilla’s impact in your local region and functional area.

If you want to learn more about our program, you might want to connect with one of our currently active Mozilla Reps in your region, so you can learn and understand the challenges and opportunities of this program.

Major requirements:

  • The applicant needs to be at least 18 years old
  • The applicant needs to be active within Mozilla for at least 6 months
