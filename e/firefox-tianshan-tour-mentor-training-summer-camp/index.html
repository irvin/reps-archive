Summer Camp

This event is a 4-week summer camp, hosted by High-tech Development Zone Government ，Xinjiang Youth League ，Xinjiang Normal University and cooperation with above Xinjiang Normal University student volunteers and other various communities in Urumqi. from July 4, 2016 and we had been invited to provide 10 classes around Firefox and programming. ，，

Location

High-tech Development Zone Government 

Date

July 3 - September 30

Participant

100 students (aged 6-14)

12-15 volunteer mentors from above training

2 Firefox Xinjiang Community Mozillians

Content plan The main theme of the curriculum will be "How to use Firefox learn a foreign language" and the content will including basic computer and internet knowledge (eg., HTML) as well as programming (scratch). Detailed plan: Curriculum of Firefox tour in Summer Camp

How these two event benefit Mozilla and community

First, the event will increase the visibility of Xinjiang Firefox Community and help us better connecting local community, schools and education department.

Secondly, we will recruiting and train 10-15 students from Xinjiang Normal University to become mentors. They will finally become our seeds in many primary schools in future and we hope at lease 1/3 of them to become Firefox Student Ambassador.

Those students who joined summer camp will learn many knowledge which they cannot easily learned from school, including computer hardware, software, copyright, net security, privacy and how to programming by scratch.

We think the event will attracting local government departments (we had already in contact with them) and help us carry out more campus events in future.

Become a Community Coordinator in Your Region!

The Mozilla Reps program is open to all Mozillians who are 18 years of age and above. Before you become a Mozilla Rep, you must follow our learning process so that you can become a good Community Coordinator in your region.

Community Coordinators are not a power structure over other Mozillians, instead they are here to help, empower and motivate other Mozillians to contribute to Mozilla projects.

There are tons of opportunities you can benefit from joining this program:

  • Access to a global community which includes people from many different countries and cultural backgrounds.
  • Direct support from Mozilla to help you empower others in your region to advance Mozilla’s goals.
  • Opportunity to help your local community grow.
  • Grow your leadership and communication skills and build networks with new people from many different countries around the globe.
  • Amplify Mozilla’s impact in your local region and functional area.

If you want to learn more about our program, you might want to connect with one of our currently active Mozilla Reps in your region, so you can learn and understand the challenges and opportunities of this program.

Find Mozilla Reps from your region

Major requirements:

  • The applicant needs to be at least 18 years old
  • The applicant needs to be active within Mozilla for at least 6 months
